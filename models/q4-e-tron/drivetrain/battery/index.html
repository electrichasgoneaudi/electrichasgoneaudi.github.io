Audi Q4 e-tron battery

The MY2027 Audi Q4 e-tron is available with 63 or 82 kWh gross battery capacity.

Last updated Jul 15, 2026
Audi Q4 e-tron Variants Dimensions Exterior Specifications Interior Drivetrain Tranportation Capabilities Technology Multimedia Reviews Knowledge exchange Options Guide

The updated Audi Q4 e-tron and Q4 Sportback e-tron use two liquid-cooled high-voltage battery sizes.

BatteryUsable capacityApplication
63 kWh gross59 kWh150 kW rear-wheel-drive versions
82 kWh gross77 kWh210 kW performance and 220/250 kW quattro versions

Gross capacity is the battery’s total nominal energy. Usable capacity is the portion made available to the driver; a buffer protects the battery at the upper and lower ends of its state-of-charge range.

DC fast charging

Maximum DC power depends on the drivetrain configuration, not only the battery size.

VersionMaximum DC charging power
150 kW rear-wheel drive160 kW
210 kW performance165 kW
220 kW quattro165 kW
250 kW quattro performance185 kW

Audi specifies about 27 minutes from 10 to 80 percent for selected versions under ideal conditions. The Q4 e-tron can precondition the battery automatically or manually before fast charging. Actual performance depends on battery temperature, state of charge, charger capability and ambient conditions.

Bidirectional use

From MY2027, the Q4 e-tron supports vehicle-to-load (V2L), and vehicle-to-home (V2H) in supported markets with compatible equipment. During V2L or V2H operation, Audi limits the discharge window to a state of charge between 20 and 80 percent.

See charging equipment for requirements and market limitations.

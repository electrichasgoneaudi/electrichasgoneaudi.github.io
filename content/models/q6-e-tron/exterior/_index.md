The Q6 e-tron translates Audi's electric design language into two body styles. Both use an inverted, largely closed Singleframe, separate daytime-running-light and headlight elements, pronounced quattro shoulders and a full-width rear light strip.

## SUV and Sportback

The SUV has an upright roofline and prioritizes luggage capacity and rear headroom. The Sportback's roof is **37 mm lower** and tapers toward a more aerodynamic rear, reducing the drag coefficient from approximately **0.28 to 0.26** in comparable configurations.

Short overhangs and a 2,899 mm wheelbase give both versions balanced proportions. The contrasting lower body treatment visually marks the battery's position between the axles.

## Exterior packages

The standard exterior, S line and SQ6 each use different bumper, Singleframe and trim treatments. S line emphasizes the air inlets and lower body, while SQ6 adds S-specific details and aluminum-look accents. Black styling packages replace selected bright trim and Audi rings with dark finishes. Exact combinations vary by market and model year.

## Paint and wheels

The launch palette included solid and metallic finishes such as Magnet Gray, Glacier White, Mythos Black, Plasma Blue, Ascari Blue and Daytona Gray. Paint availability changes by market, and some finishes are restricted to specific equipment lines.

Wheel sizes range from **18 to 21 inches**. The regular Q6 starts with 18-inch wheels in many markets, S line commonly uses 19-inch designs, and SQ6 uses 20-inch wheels as standard. Larger wheels can reduce range and alter ride comfort.

## Lighting

Depending on equipment, Matrix LED headlights and second-generation digital OLED rear lights offer selectable light signatures. The digital rear lights can also display warning symbols to communicate hazards to following traffic.

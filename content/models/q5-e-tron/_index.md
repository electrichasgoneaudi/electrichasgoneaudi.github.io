The **Audi Q5 e-tron** is a **China-exclusive, fully electric, three-row SUV** built on the **MEB platform**. Closely related to the Volkswagen ID.6, it is positioned above the Q4 e-tron in Audi's China lineup and combines a 2,965 mm wheelbase with six- or seven-seat configurations. Buyers can choose rear-wheel or all-wheel drive.

The Q5 e-tron had its **world premiere on 18 November 2021** and is produced and sold exclusively in China.

---

## Variants

Two variants are available:

| Variant | Power | Drive | 0–100 km/h | CLTC Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Q5 e-tron 40** | 150 kW (204 hp) | Rear-wheel drive | 9.3 s | 560 km |
| **Q5 e-tron 50 quattro** | 225 kW (306 hp) | All-wheel drive | 6.7 s | 550 km |

Both variants share the same **83.4 kWh (gross) / 76.6 kWh (net) NMC battery** and are limited to a top speed of 160 km/h.

## Design and dimensions

The Q5 e-tron follows Audi's current design language with a clean, progressive SUV profile. The extended wheelbase gives it a commanding presence on the road.

Key dimensions:

- Length: **4,876 mm**
- Width: **1,860 mm** (excluding mirrors)
- Height: **1,675 mm**
- Wheelbase: **2,965 mm**

## Interior

The Q5 e-tron is available with either six seats in a 2+2+2 arrangement or seven seats in a 2+3+2 arrangement. The cabin combines Audi virtual cockpit with an MMI touchscreen, while selected equipment lines add features such as front-seat ventilation and massage functions.

→ [Full interior details: seats, screens and features](./interior/)

## Drivetrain

Both variants use the same **83.4 kWh NMC battery pack**, supported by the MEB platform's proven electric architecture. The suspension combines a **MacPherson strut front** with a **multi-link rear**, and optional adaptive damping is available.

The charge port is located on the **left rear side** of the vehicle.

The Audi A6 e-tron combines cameras, radar sensors and ultrasonic sensors with a central driver-assistance control unit. Depending on equipment, up to five radar sensors, five cameras and 12 ultrasonic sensors monitor the vehicle's surroundings.

At its European launch, the standard assistance equipment included:

- Audi pre sense front with collision warning and emergency braking
- Lane-departure warning
- Adaptive cruise control and a traffic-sign-based speed limiter
- Camera-based traffic-sign recognition
- Park assist plus with distance display and a reversing camera
- Distraction and drowsiness warning

The optional adaptive driving assistant plus can support acceleration, braking, distance control and lane guidance. Depending on region, it can combine sensor information with high-resolution map and cloud-based swarm data. Additional equipment can include surround-view cameras, side assist, exit warning and rear cross-traffic assist.

Availability and package names vary by country, variant and model year. The driver remains responsible for monitoring traffic and intervening when required.
